The University Today

A Modern European University

National University "Yuri Kondratyuk Poltava Polytechnic" is a comprehensive European-style university located in the heart of Poltava, Ukraine. Named after the visionary space theoretician Yuri Kondratyuk, whose gravitational slingshot calculations were used by NASA in the Apollo program, NUPP carries a proud legacy of innovation.

With 8 structural divisions, 94 educational programs spanning arts, humanities, law, finance, and technology, and approximately 10,000 total staff — the university serves as a cornerstone of higher education in central Ukraine.

A signatory of the Magna Charta Universitatum and an active participant in Erasmus+ and Erasmus Mundus programs, NUPP is firmly integrated into the European academic community.

Our History

90+ Years of Excellence

From a small engineering institute to a comprehensive national university — NUPP's story is one of resilience, growth, and ambition.

1818

Historical Roots

The Institute for Noble Girls established on December 12, 1818. Princess Varvara Riepnina founded this prestigious institution at a landmark donated by Semen Kochubei. Poet Ivan Kotliarevskyi and painter Taras Shevchenko (June–July 1845) are among its notable visitors.

1930

Engineering Institute Founded

August 19, 1930: The People's Commissariat for Agriculture establishes the Poltava Institute for Agricultural Construction and Engineering. Five departments, 33 lecturers, 282 students — housed in the historic Noble Girls' building.

1941–1945

War and Reconstruction

The institute evacuates to Uralsk, Kazakhstan during WWII. Over 2,000 civil engineers graduate during the war years; 267 staff and students serve militarily. Senior lecturer D.M. Batishchev famously raised the victory flag in Berlin, May 1945.

1961

Renamed Poltava Civil Engineering Institute

Ministry Decree 12 renames the institution. Enrollment grows to over 500 students, with new faculties in industrial engineering, architecture, sanitary engineering, and advanced study programs.

1994

Technical University Status

August 29, 1994: Renamed Poltava Technical University, achieving IV-level accreditation and expanding from 6 to 26 specializations — beginning the transformation into a comprehensive institution.

1997

Named for Yuri Kondratyuk

June 21, 1997: The university is renamed in honor of space theoretician Yuri Kondratyuk, on the centennial of his birth. Kondratyuk's gravitational slingshot calculations were foundational to the Apollo lunar mission.

2002

National University

March 27, 2002: Designated National Technical University status by presidential decree, recognizing the institution's research output, faculty quality, and national significance.

2019

Poltava Polytechnic

August 2, 2019: Final renaming to "National University Yuri Kondratyuk Poltava Polytechnic," reflecting the institution's broad multidisciplinary European identity. 94 programs now span arts, humanities, law, finance, and technology.

Institutes & Faculties

Eight structural divisions cover the full breadth of modern academic disciplines.

Institute

Architecture, Civil Engineering & Land Management

Engineering, construction, architecture, and geospatial sciences — the university's founding faculty, now one of Ukraine's strongest.

Institute

Oil, Gas & Nature Engineering

Drilling engineering, pipeline management, environmental technology, and natural resource sciences. Strong ties to the Ukrainian energy industry.

Computer science, software engineering, robotics, AI, and cybersecurity. Home to the university's 3D modeling labs and startup school (est. 2018).
