§ 01 — Campus Spots

The places
students actually hang out.

Eight stories tall, two centuries old, and somehow always smelling like fresh coffee from the cafeteria — here's what makes our campus tick. Six spots every NUPP student knows by heart.

Block 01 · Pershotravneva Ave

The Main Building

The 1930s temple of engineering — eight floors, marble stairs, and the loudest hallway echo in Poltava. Selfies on the front steps are mandatory.
The Dorms · 6 buildings

The Dorms

Six buildings, one shared kettle. Where 4 a.m. exam crams meet 4 p.m. hallway dance battles. Bring slippers — it's a rule.
Library

Scientific Library

1.2M volumes. The good study spot is by the window on the third floor — don't tell anyone.
Sport Complex

The Stadium

Football, athletics, the annual Faculty Cup. Also: best place to watch the sunset over Poltava.
Park

The Student Park

Old chestnuts, painted benches, a fountain that works two months a year. Date spot, nap spot, debate spot.

§ 06 — Newcomers' FAQ

Questions you
haven't asked yet.

The honest answers — from a cohort of upperclassmen who remember exactly how confusing week one was.

Where do I actually live in first year?+
First-years get priority placement in dorms 3, 4, and 5 (closest to the main building). Rooms are typically 2-3 person, kitchen and showers shared by floor. Bring a lamp, slippers, and a tea kettle — the rest sorts itself out by week two.
Is everything taught in Ukrainian?+
Mostly yes. Several master's tracks (IT, Civil Engineering, Architecture) run in English. International students also get free Ukrainian language classes for the first two semesters.
What's the food situation?+
Three cafeterias on campus, plus a couple of student-run coffee corners. Lunch averages 80-120 UAH. Pro tip: the Block 02 cafeteria does a borscht on Wednesdays that has its own fan club.
How do I join a club?+
There's a Club Day in the second week of September — every society sets up a stand outside the Main Building. Just turn up, sign your name, eat whatever they're offering. You can also DM most clubs directly through their Telegram channels.
Can I work part-time while studying?+
Yes — and most students do, especially from the second year. The Career Centre helps match students to local internships and remote roles. Many IT-faculty students freelance from week one.
Where do students go on weekends?+
Round Square (Kruhla Ploshcha), the Vorskla embankment, the corpus park, the gallery district, and — when nostalgia kicks in mid-semester — straight back to the dorm kitchen for a 1 a.m. tea council.
