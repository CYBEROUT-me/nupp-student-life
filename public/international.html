Student Story

From Poltava to Ceprano: Yelizavieta's Erasmus+ Journey

Third-year philology student Yelizavieta Samoilenko took part in the Erasmus+ "Peace and Youth" exchange program held March 2–6, 2026, in Ceprano, Italy. The initiative brought together young people from Ukraine, France, Italy, and Poland to explore themes of war, peace, and democratic values.

Workshops on democratic principles, historical discussions about World War II, and a field trip to Cassino and Monte Cassino monastery were among the program's highlights. Cultural evenings allowed participants to share their national traditions — the Ukrainian delegation presented both their country's modern challenges and cultural heritage.

"This project was not only an opportunity to travel for me but also a valuable experience of communicating with people from different countries."

— Yelizavieta Samoilenko, Philology, Year 3

How It Works

Applying for an Exchange

01

Find a Program

Browse open calls on the NUPP website or check with the International Relations Office in room 213-C.

02

Check Eligibility

Confirm you meet language requirements (usually B2 English or German), academic standing, and year of study.

03

Prepare Documents

Transcript, motivation letter, language certificate, and recommendation from your faculty. Allow 2–3 weeks.

04

Submit & Wait

Submit your application to interoffice@nupp.edu.ua. Decisions are made within 4–6 weeks by the partner university.

International Relations Office

Have questions? Visit us in room 213-C or reach out by email. We'll guide you through every step of the application process.
