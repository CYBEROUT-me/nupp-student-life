About Sports at NUPP

Active Body, Active Mind

NUPP boasts a dedicated Faculty of Physical Culture and Sports — one of the eight academic institutes at the university. Students across all faculties are encouraged to participate in regular sporting activities, both competitive and recreational.

The campus provides two fully equipped gymnasiums, outdoor sports grounds, and organized leagues for dormitory teams, making it easy to stay active year-round.

Sports Clubs & Activities

Join a club, compete in tournaments, or just use the facilities — sport at NUPP is for everyone.

01

Mini-Football

Year-roundAll levels

The most popular sport on campus. Inter-dormitory leagues run throughout the year, with the Campus Cup held each spring. Outdoor pitches available daily.

02

Table Tennis

Daily accessChampionship: Jun

Regular championships organized by the Sports Department. Tables available in both gymnasiums and the dormitory recreation rooms at no cost to students.

03

Volleyball

Spring seasonMixed teams

Inter-faculty volleyball championships held each spring. The Faculty of Physical Culture fields especially strong teams but welcomes players from all institutes.

04

Gym & Fitness

Mon–Sat 08:00–20:00

Both university gymnasiums are open to all enrolled students. Equipped with free weights, cardio machines, and areas for group fitness. Supervised sessions available.

05

Martial Arts

3× per weekAll levels

Self-defense and martial arts training offered through the Faculty of Physical Culture. Open to all genders and experience levels. Club meets three times per week.

06

Athletics & Running

Year-roundCity events

Morning runs, track workouts, and seasonal cross-country events. The university participates in Poltava city athletic competitions and regional student sports meetings.

Annual Tournament

The Campus Cup

The Inter-Dormitory Mini-Football Campus Cup is the most anticipated sporting event of the year. All five dormitories field teams in a knockout tournament, with months of trash-talk and training leading up to the big day.

The final takes place on the main campus sports ground with spectators filling the sidelines. The winning dormitory earns bragging rights and a permanent spot on the Campus Cup trophy.
