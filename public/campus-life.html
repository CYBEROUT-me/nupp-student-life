§ 01 — A typical Tuesday

From kettle
to curfew.

Every faculty has its own rhythm, but Tuesdays look more or less like this for a second-year IT student living in Dorm 4.

07am · alarm

Kettle on, slippers on.

Block 04 wakes up slowly. Coffee from the corner kiosk costs 35 UAH and the man behind it remembers your order by week three.

The 12-minute walk.

Cut through the park, past the bronze plaque, up the front steps of the Main Building. There are exactly 23 steps — yes, students have counted.

08am · lecture

Algorithms, Aud. 312.

Old wood desks, blackboard, professor draws diagrams faster than you can copy. Front row reserved for the keen kids; back row for the recovering.

10-minute break, hallway buzz.

The corridor outside Aud. 312 is the unofficial gossip exchange. Important things heard here: club elections, exam leaks (rumours), engagement rumours (also rumours).

13pm · lunch

Cafeteria, Block 02.

The borscht queue is fastest at 13:05 sharp, before the architects finish their studio. Galushky on Wednesdays. Compote always.

Coffee on the steps.

If the sun's out, students park themselves on the front-stairs balustrade. Twenty minutes of nothing. Sacred.

15pm · lab

Computer lab, Block 03.

Linux machines, three monitors each, instant noodles smell faintly in the air. Group project time. Four people, two opinions, one merge conflict.

Library, third floor, by the window.

The good seats. Phone face-down, headphones in, two hours of finally getting things done. Old radiator clicks comfortingly.

18pm · clubs

Robotics lab — Tuesday meet.

Soldering, solving, occasionally setting off a smoke alarm. A pizza usually appears around 19:30, no one knows who orders it.

Choir rehearsal, hall 2.

Polifoniya warms up with scales, ends with someone playing a song on the piano while everyone sings. Acoustics: criminally good.

23pm · dorm

Tea council in the kitchen.

Six people, four mugs, one slightly suspicious cake from someone's mum. Topics: politics, philosophy, why the radiator on third floor whistles.

One last hour at the desk.

Lights out by 01:00 if you're sensible, 03:00 if it's exam week, and you tell yourself you'll sleep on the weekend.

§ 02 — Block 01

The Main Building has its own gravity.

Built in the 1930s on Pershotravneva Avenue, the eight-floor flagship is where every NUPP student takes at least one class — usually a lot more. Marble stairs, original chandeliers in the rector's hall, and an attic rumoured to house a piano no one has played since 1987.

§ 03 — Гуртожитки

Six dorms.
One shared kettle.

Buildings 1 through 6, all within ten minutes of the Main Building. Each has its personality — Block 03 is the noisy one, Block 05 has the legendary kitchen, Block 06 is where the international students live and learn three languages by accident.
